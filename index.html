Tournament format

48 teams in 12 groups of four, playing a round-robin. The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams advance to a 32-team knockout bracket: R32, R16, quarterfinals, semifinals, third-place play-off, and final. 104 matches across 39 days.

Points

Win = 3  |  Draw = 1  |  Loss = 0

Group-stage tiebreakers (FIFA Regulations)

Two teams level on points are separated by:

  1. Goal difference (all group matches)
  2. Goals scored (all group matches)
  3. Head-to-head: points in the match between them
  4. Head-to-head: goal difference
  5. Head-to-head: goals scored
  6. Fair-play score (cards)
  7. FIFA World Ranking

Three or more teams level on points: a head-to-head mini-league is applied first (points, GD, then goals scored in the matches among those teams). If some teams separate, the remaining tied teams restart the process. After head-to-head is exhausted, the overall criteria above apply (GD, GF, fair-play, ranking).

This tracker automates criteria 1 through 5, including the head-to-head mini-league for three-way ties. Fair-play and FIFA ranking are not tracked; any ties that reach those criteria are flagged for manual resolution.

Third-place ranking

All 12 third-placed teams are ranked by points, goal difference, goals scored, fair-play, FIFA ranking. The top 8 advance. Their R32 opponents are assigned via a FIFA matrix based on which 8 groups they come from.

Knockout tiebreaker

30 minutes of extra time (2 × 15 min). If still level, penalty shootout.

Bracket structure

The draw is designed so the top two seeded teams (Spain and Argentina) cannot meet before the final if both win their groups. Same for France and England.
