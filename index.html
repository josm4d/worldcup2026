Format

48 teams, 12 groups of four, round-robin. Top two per group plus the 8 best third-placed teams reach the Round of 32, then straight knockout to the final. 104 matches across 39 days.

Points

Win 3 · Draw 1 · Loss 0

Group tiebreakers (FIFA Regulations)

Two teams level on points:

  1. Goal difference (all group matches)
  2. Goals scored (all group matches)
  3. Head-to-head points
  4. Head-to-head goal difference
  5. Head-to-head goals scored
  6. Fair-play score
  7. FIFA World Ranking

Three or more teams level: head-to-head mini-league first (points, GD, goals among them). Teams that separate are placed; the rest restart the process. After head-to-head is exhausted, overall criteria apply.

This tracker automates criteria 1–5 including the head-to-head mini-league. Fair-play (criterion 6) is applied automatically if you enter each team's fair-play points on the relevant matches — there's a small optional field on every fixture. Only the final FIFA-ranking tiebreaker is left for you to judge, and any tie reaching that point is flagged in the standings.

Fair-play scoring (criterion 6)

If teams remain level after head-to-head and overall GD/GF, the team with the better conduct score ranks higher. Cards for both players and team officials count. Points are deducted as follows:

Yellow card−1
Indirect red (second yellow)−3
Direct red card−4
Yellow then direct red (same player)−5

The team with the higher (less negative) total is ranked above. This decided Japan over Senegal in 2018 — identical on every other measure, Senegal had more yellow cards. If still tied after fair-play, the most recent FIFA World Ranking is the final tiebreaker.

Third place

All 12 third-placed teams ranked by points, GD, goals scored, fair-play, FIFA ranking. Top 8 advance; their R32 slot follows FIFA's allocation matrix.

Knockout

30 minutes extra time, then penalties. Record shootouts in the PEN boxes.

Bracket design

Argentina and Spain (top two seeds in the June FIFA ranking) cannot meet before the final if both win their groups; the same applies to France and England.

Team rankings & win probability

Each team's FIFA World Ranking (11 June 2026, final pre-tournament update) is shown beside its name. The win-probability bar on each fixture is a model estimate derived from those rankings using an Elo-style formula. It is an indicative guide, not bookmaker odds, and does not account for form, injuries, or squad changes.

Kickoff times

All official kickoffs are set in US Eastern Time. This tracker shows the venue's local time and converts to your device's timezone automatically.
